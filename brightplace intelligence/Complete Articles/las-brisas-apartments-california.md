# Las Brisas Apartments in California: Which One Are You Looking For?

## Multiple Properties in California Share the Las Brisas Name

Las Brisas apartments California refers to at least eight independent apartment communities spread across the state, with no shared ownership or management between them. "Las Brisas" is a Spanish phrase meaning "the breezes," and renters searching this name will encounter properties ranging from market-rate coastal communities to income-restricted affordable housing. brightplace tracks apartment search patterns across California, and "Las Brisas" is one of the most searched community names that returns inconsistent results because of shared naming.

The properties span South Los Angeles, Santa Maria, Colton, Chula Vista, Huntington Beach, Indio, El Centro, and Cudahy. Each has different management, pricing, unit types, and qualification requirements. The comparison table below breaks down what distinguishes each one.

## Las Brisas Apartments in the Los Angeles Area

Three properties in the greater Los Angeles area use the Las Brisas name, and they serve very different renter profiles.

The South Los Angeles property at 8760 Main Street (ZIP 90003) is a courtyard-style community managed by Cirrus Asset Management. Amenities include a computer lab, playground, and subterranean parking. This is a market-rate property, so standard income and credit qualifications apply. When contacting the leasing office, confirm whether the property is income-restricted before applying. Qualification requirements for LIHTC properties differ substantially from market-rate leasing, and the confusion is common.

Las Brisas I & II in the Cudahy and South LA area is an entirely different category. Developed by Abode Communities, this is a **LIHTC** (Low Income Housing Tax Credit) affordable housing community with 152 total homes: 92 in Phase I and 60 in Phase II. The development includes a community center, childcare facility, and Cabrisas Park. Residents must meet income qualifications, with the 60% AMI threshold for a 4-person household in LA County set at $75,660/year (as of current HUD data). Housing vouchers may be accepted.

## Income-Restricted Las Brisas Communities in California

Not all Las Brisas properties charge market-rate rent. Several participate in the **LIHTC** program, the Low Income Housing Tax Credit, a federal initiative that provides subsidies to developers in exchange for capping rents at levels affordable to households below a percentage of **Area Median Income (AMI)**, the midpoint income for a given geographic area published annually by <a href="https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/comm_planning/affordablehousing/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">HUD</a>.

The El Centro property at 2001 North 8th Street in Imperial County is managed by ConAm Management and offers 1- to 3-bedroom units ranging from 734 to 1,136 square feet. The 60% AMI income threshold for a 4-person household in Imperial County is $49,500/year (as of current HUD data). This property accepts Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers.

The Cudahy property at 4343 Elizabeth Street, managed by Hyder Property Management, also operates under LIHTC guidelines with 1- to 3-bedroom units. The Abode Communities Las Brisas I & II development serves families and special needs populations.

Under federal law, all LIHTC properties are required to accept Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers. ## California Renter Protections That Apply at Any Las Brisas Property

California renters have specific legal protections regardless of which Las Brisas property they choose. Three laws are particularly relevant.

**AB 1482 (Tenant Protection Act of 2019).** This law caps annual rent increases at 5% plus local CPI, with a maximum of 10%, for most residential buildings 15 years or older. The Santa Maria property (built 2024/2026) is too new to qualify currently. Older properties in LA, Colton, and Chula Vista likely fall under AB 1482. The full text is available through the <a href="https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=201920200AB1482" target="_blank" rel="noopener">California Legislative Information site</a>.

**AB 12 (Security Deposit Reform, effective July 1, 2024).** Security deposits for unfurnished units are now capped at one month's rent (as of Q2 2026). Previously, the cap was two months for unfurnished units. This is a direct financial benefit for renters signing new leases at any Las Brisas community.

**Just Cause Eviction (AB 1482).** Most multi-unit buildings in California require a documented reason for eviction after 12 months of tenancy. Renters in any Las Brisas community with 12 or more months of occupancy have additional lease security under this provision.

## Common Questions About Las Brisas Apartments in California

### What are Las Brisas apartments in California?

"Las Brisas" is a name shared by at least eight independent apartment communities across California, including properties in South Los Angeles, Santa Maria, Colton, Chula Vista, Huntington Beach, Indio, El Centro, and Cudahy. They have no common ownership or management. The name comes from the Spanish phrase for "the breezes." To find the right property, search by city name alongside "Las Brisas."

### Are there low-income Las Brisas apartments in California?

Yes, several Las Brisas properties participate in LIHTC income-restricted programs. The El Centro property at 2001 N 8th Street and the Cudahy property at 4343 Elizabeth Street are both LIHTC communities. Abode Communities Las Brisas I & II in the South LA area serves families and special needs populations. These properties accept Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers.

### Where are Las Brisas apartments in Los Angeles?

Two properties in the broader Los Angeles area use the Las Brisas name. One is at 8760 Main Street in South Los Angeles (ZIP 90003), managed by Cirrus Asset Management. The other is the Abode Communities Las Brisas I & II development in the South LA/Cudahy area, which provides affordable housing for families under the LIHTC program.

### What is Las Brisas apartments in Santa Maria?

Las Brisas Santa Maria is a 340-unit, garden-style apartment community in Santa Barbara County, developed by MBK Rental Living and managed by Greystar. It opened for leasing in early 2026 and offers 1-, 2-, and 3-bedroom floor plans with amenities including a resort-style pool, 24-hour fitness center, dog spa, and co-working spaces.

### Where are Las Brisas apartments in Colton?

Las Brisas Colton is located at 2001 N Rancho Ave, Colton, CA 92324. The property is managed by Western National Property Management and offers studio and 1-bedroom floor plans. It is a garden-style community in the Inland Empire with outdoor picnic and BBQ areas. The leasing office phone number is 909-572-8951.

### What does a Las Brisas apartment in California cost?

Rent varies entirely by location and property type. In Chula Vista, 3-bedroom units range from $2,695 to $2,950 per month (as of Q2 2026). In Huntington Beach, 2-bedroom units are listed around $2,625 to $2,795 per month (as of Q2 2026). Income-restricted properties in El Centro and Cudahy price units based on Area Median Income limits; contact those properties directly for current rates.

### Do Las Brisas apartments in California accept pets?

Pet policies vary by property. The Chula Vista Las Brisas Del Mar accepts cats and dogs with a maximum weight of 40 pounds, a $500 pet deposit (as of Q2 2026), and $50 per month pet rent. The Colton property also lists as pet-friendly. The Santa Maria property includes a dog park and dog spa on site. Always confirm current breed restrictions and deposit amounts directly with the leasing office.

### Are Las Brisas apartments in California Section 8 approved?

Some Las Brisas properties accept Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers. Under federal law, all LIHTC properties are required to accept vouchers. The El Centro and Cudahy properties are confirmed LIHTC communities that accept Section 8. Market-rate Las Brisas properties in Santa Maria, Huntington Beach, Colton, and Chula Vista are not LIHTC-designated; contact those leasing offices directly to confirm voucher acceptance policies.
