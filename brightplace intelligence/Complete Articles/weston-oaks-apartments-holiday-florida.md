# Weston Oaks Apartments in Holiday, FL: What Renters Should Know

## What Is Weston Oaks Apartments?

Weston Oaks Apartments Florida is an income-restricted rental community in Holiday, FL, offering 200 units under the [Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC)](https://www.huduser.gov/portal/datasets/lihtc.html) program at the 60% Area Median Income level. The property includes one-, two-, and three-bedroom floor plans and is managed by Highmark Residential.

The community is located at 1251 Weston Oaks Drive, Holiday, FL 34691. The leasing office can be reached at 727-334-0493. Holiday is an unincorporated community in Pasco County, roughly 30 miles north of Tampa along the Gulf Coast. This is not the city of Weston in Broward County, which is a common point of confusion when searching for this property online.

Weston Oaks contains 200 total units: 40 one-bedroom, 96 two-bedroom, and 64 three-bedroom. Two-bedroom layouts make up nearly half the community. All units fall under LIHTC income restrictions, meaning applicants must meet household income thresholds before signing a lease.

## Floor Plans and Unit Sizes

Weston Oaks offers three floor plan types. Rents and square footage break down as follows:

**1 Bedroom / 1 Bathroom:** 651 sq ft, starting at $1,219/month (as of 2025-2026 leasing data). This is the smallest layout and the least available, with only 40 units in the community.

**2 Bedroom / 2 Bathroom:** 926 sq ft, starting at $1,200/month (as of 2025-2026 leasing data). The two-bedroom starts lower than the one-bedroom. This reflects how LIHTC rent caps are calculated by bedroom count and local AMI thresholds, not by unit square footage. It is not a pricing error.

**3 Bedroom / 2 Bathroom:** 1,089 sq ft, starting at $1,400/month (as of 2025-2026 leasing data). These are the largest units on property and account for 64 of the 200 total apartments.

All rents are set under federal LIHTC program rules and may adjust annually based on updated AMI figures published by HUD. ## Income Eligibility and How the 60% AMI Program Works

Households at Weston Oaks must earn no more than 60% of the Pasco County Area Median Income to qualify, with a four-person household capped at $42,180 annually (HUD 2025 AMI).

**LIHTC (Low-Income Housing Tax Credit)** is a federal program that gives tax credits to developers who build or renovate apartments and reserve them for renters below certain income thresholds. **AMI (Area Median Income)** is the midpoint household income for a given county or metro area, calculated and published annually by the [U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD)](https://www.hud.gov/). Weston Oaks operates at the **60% AMI** level, meaning tenants must earn no more than 60% of the Pasco County area median income to qualify.

Current income limits for 60% AMI in Pasco County (HUD 2025):

- **1 person:** $29,580
- **2 persons:** $33,780
- **3 persons:** $37,980
- **4 persons:** $42,180
- **5 persons:** $45,600
- **6 persons:** $48,960
- **7 persons:** $52,320
- **8 persons:** $55,680

These limits are based on gross household income and apply to all adult members listed on the lease. Income is verified at initial move-in and again each year through an annual recertification process. If your income rises above the threshold after move-in, you may still retain your unit under certain LIHTC rules, but the specifics depend on how far above the limit your household falls.

One important distinction: LIHTC is not the same as [Section 8 (Housing Choice Vouchers)](https://www.hud.gov/topics/housing_choice_voucher_program_section_8). Section 8 is a direct rental subsidy where the government pays a portion of your rent to the landlord. LIHTC sets maximum rent amounts for the unit itself, but the tenant pays the full listed rent. You can hold a Section 8 voucher and apply it at a LIHTC property if the landlord accepts vouchers, but the two programs operate independently.

There are no breed restrictions, though the property reserves the right to decline animals with a documented history of aggression or biting.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Weston Oaks Apartments

### What are the income limits to rent at Weston Oaks?

Weston Oaks uses the 60% AMI standard for Pasco County. For a single person, the 2025 limit is $29,580 per year. A four-person household can earn up to $42,180. These figures come from HUD and are updated annually. All adult household members must submit income verification before lease signing.

### What floor plan sizes are available at Weston Oaks?

Weston Oaks offers one-bedroom (651 sq ft), two-bedroom (926 sq ft), and three-bedroom (1,089 sq ft) layouts. Rents start at $1,200/month for a two-bedroom and $1,219/month for a one-bedroom (as of 2025-2026 leasing data). Two-bedroom units are the most common, with 96 of the 200 total apartments.

### Are pets allowed at Weston Oaks Apartments?

Yes. Weston Oaks permits up to two cats or dogs per unit. The first pet carries a $300 non-refundable fee, and the second costs $200 (as of 2025-2026 leasing data). Monthly pet rent is $20 per pet, plus a $100 refundable deposit. No breed restrictions, though animals with documented aggression or bite history may be declined.

### Is Weston Oaks a Section 8 or subsidized housing property?

Weston Oaks is a LIHTC property, not a Section 8 community. LIHTC caps the rent amount on each unit based on AMI thresholds, but tenants pay the full listed rent themselves. Section 8 vouchers may be accepted if the landlord participates, but the two programs are separate. Contact the leasing office at 727-334-0493 for voucher acceptance details.

### How do I apply to Weston Oaks Apartments in Holiday, FL?

Applications are submitted online through Highmark Residential's SecureCafe portal. You will need income documentation for every adult in the household, including pay stubs and tax returns. Call 727-334-0493 to confirm availability or ask about the waitlist before starting an application.

### What is the difference between 60% AMI rent and market rate rent?

Rent at a 60% AMI property like Weston Oaks is capped by HUD formulas tied to area income data. Market rate apartments have no such cap and price units based on demand, location, and amenities. In many Florida markets, 60% AMI rents run noticeably below comparable market rate units, though the gap varies by county and year.
