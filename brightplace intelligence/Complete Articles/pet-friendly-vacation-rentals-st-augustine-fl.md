# How to Find Pet-Friendly Vacation Rentals in St. Augustine, FL

Pet-friendly vacation rentals in St. Augustine, FL are widely available, but the term "pet-friendly" covers a broad range of actual policies. If you are traveling to St. Augustine with a dog or cat, this guide covers what to check, what to pay, and where options are most plentiful. Pet fees typically run $50 to $150 per stay for one dog (as of Q2 2026), and policies vary considerably between standalone houses and condo complexes. Knowing the differences before you book saves money and prevents unpleasant surprises at check-in.

## What Pet-Friendly Actually Means in St. Augustine Rentals

### Non-Refundable Pet Fees

Most pet-friendly vacation rentals in St. Augustine charge a **non-refundable pet fee**, a one-time charge added to the rental cost to cover additional cleaning related to pets. This fee is not returned at checkout regardless of property condition. Fees in this market typically range from $50 to $150 per stay for one dog (as of Q2 2026). Some properties charge $75 to $100 per additional pet. The label "pet-friendly" does not guarantee uniform policies across properties.

### Owner Permissions vs. HOA Rules

The most important distinction is between owner-level permissions and community-level rules. A condo owner may list a unit as pet-friendly, but the **HOA (homeowners association)**, the governing body for a condo or resort community, can set rules that override individual owner preferences. Some HOAs in St. Augustine prohibit pets entirely, even when a listing says otherwise.

### Dogs vs. Cats in Listings

"Dogs allowed" and "cats allowed" are also separate categories in many listings. Several St. Augustine properties accept dogs but require a special request for cats, or vice versa. Confirm your specific pet type before booking.

## What to Check Before Booking Any Pet-Friendly Rental

The most common booking mistake we see with St. Augustine pet rentals is assuming the listing filter is comprehensive. Community HOA rules can prohibit pets regardless of what the individual owner states in the listing. Before committing to any property, verify the following points directly with the property manager:

**1. Weight and breed limits.** Many St. Augustine rentals cap pet weight at 25 to 50 pounds. **Breed restrictions**, policies that prohibit specific dog breeds from staying at a rental, are typically enforced at the community or HOA level. Breeds commonly excluded by multiple resort communities in this market include:

- Pit bulls
- Rottweilers
- German Shepherds
- Dobermans

Ask about breed-specific rules even if the listing does not mention them.

**2. Number of pets.** Some resort communities in St. Augustine limit guests to one pet per rental, strictly enforced at check-in. If you are traveling with two dogs, confirm the pet count policy before paying any deposit.

**3. Fee structure.** Clarify whether the property charges a flat pet fee or a nightly surcharge. These are very different costs for a week-long stay.

**4. Fenced yard availability.** A **fenced yard**, an enclosed outdoor area where dogs can be off-leash safely, is a filterable amenity on major short-term rental platforms and one of the highest-value filters for dog owners. Fenced yards are available at a subset of St. Augustine pet-friendly rentals, primarily standalone houses in Anastasia Island and Davis Shores.

**5. HOA confirmation.** For any condo rental, contact the property management company directly. Do not rely solely on the listing description.

## Pet Fees and What to Expect on Your Final Bill

### Flat Fees vs. Nightly Surcharges

One-time non-refundable pet fees in the St. Augustine market typically range from $50 to $150 for one dog (as of Q2 2026). Properties charging nightly surcharges typically add $25 to $50 per night (as of Q2 2026). On a seven-night stay, a $35/night surcharge adds $245 to your bill, compared to a flat $100 fee. The difference matters.

### Refundable Deposits vs. Non-Refundable Fees

Some properties charge a refundable pet damage deposit instead of, or in addition to, the non-refundable fee. A refundable deposit is returned after checkout if no pet-related damage is found. Confirm which structure applies before booking so you can compare properties accurately.

A pet fee does not guarantee the property is fully prepared for pets. Ask specifically about outdoor pet cleanup access, waste bag availability, and whether pets are restricted from certain rooms or areas. Understanding [your true monthly cost](https://brightplace.ai/guides/your-true-monthly-cost) matters for longer stays, where fees compound.

## Dog-Friendly Beaches and Parks Near Your Rental

### On-Leash Beaches

All <a href="https://www.sjcfl.us/Beaches" target="_blank" rel="noopener">St. Johns County beaches</a> permit leashed dogs, with the exception of the beach section within Anastasia State Park. Dogs are not allowed on the state park beach itself, though leashed dogs can use designated trails and day-use areas within <a href="https://www.floridastateparks.org/parks-and-trails/anastasia-state-park" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Anastasia State Park</a>.

Vilano Beach welcomes dogs on leash at all times, including in the water. Cleanup is required. St. Augustine Beach along the A1A corridor permits leashed dogs, with waste bag stations at several beach parking lot entrances. Crescent Beach also permits leashed dogs.

### Off-Leash Dog Parks

For off-leash exercise, <a href="https://www.sjcfl.us/Parks/TreatyPark" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Treaty Park</a> is the closest option for stays in the western or downtown areas. It has two fenced areas separating small and large dogs. Ron Parker Park near St. Augustine Beach is the closest off-leash option for beach-area rental stays.

## Comparing Rental Types: Houses vs. Condos for Pet Owners

### Standalone Houses and Cottages

Standalone houses and cottages are the most reliable property type for pet owners in St. Augustine. The owner sets the policy directly. Houses are more likely to have fenced yards and direct outdoor access, both of which make a stay with dogs significantly easier.

### Condos in Resort Communities

Condos in resort communities present a different situation. Communities with their own pet policies include:

- Ocean Village Club
- Barefoot Trace
- Beacher's Lodge
- St. Augustine Beach and Tennis Resort

Always confirm with the property manager at the community level, not just the listing level.

### Pool Homes

Pool homes are common on Anastasia Island and in St. Augustine Beach. Pets are typically restricted from the pool area across virtually all listings. Verify that the property has outdoor space separate from the pool for your dog.

### Downtown Historic Rentals

Downtown historic rentals are often converted bungalows with limited outdoor space. These work for smaller or calmer dogs but may not suit larger, more active breeds. If you are planning a trip to explore [dog-friendly neighborhoods](https://brightplace.ai/guides/dog-friendly-neighborhoods-san-diego) as part of a possible relocation, St. Augustine's beach neighborhoods offer the most space.

## Finding Pet-Friendly Vacation Rentals with Pools in St. Augustine

Pool homes that also accept pets are available in St. Augustine, concentrated in Anastasia Island and St. Augustine Beach (as of Q2 2026). Filter for both "pet-friendly" and "private pool" on your chosen vacation rental site.

Expect the pet to be restricted from the pool deck and pool area in nearly all cases. Properties with a pool and a separate fenced yard offer the most practical setup for dog owners. Nightly rates for pet-friendly pool homes in these areas typically fall between $200 and $400 per night (as of Q2 2026), depending on season and property size.

## Oceanfront Pet-Friendly Rentals in St. Augustine

Oceanfront pet-friendly vacation rentals are available in St. Augustine, with the highest concentration along St. Augustine Beach, Vilano Beach, and Crescent Beach. Standalone oceanfront houses carry fewer pet policy complications than oceanfront condos.

Oceanfront condo rentals come with elevated HOA restriction risk. Several beachfront condo communities in St. Augustine either prohibit pets entirely or impose strict weight limits. Confirm community-level rules before booking any oceanfront condo. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Are there pet-friendly vacation rentals in St. Augustine with no pet fee?

A small number of St. Augustine vacation rentals waive pet fees entirely, though this is uncommon. Properties that skip the fee typically build pet-related cleaning costs into the base nightly rate. Search listings carefully and read the full fee disclosure before assuming a property is truly fee-free. Always confirm directly with the property manager.

### 2. Can my dog go on the beach in St. Augustine?

Yes. All St. Johns County beaches allow leashed dogs, including Vilano Beach, St. Augustine Beach, and Crescent Beach. The one exception is the beach within Anastasia State Park, where dogs are not permitted on the sand. Leashed dogs can use trails and day-use areas within the state park grounds.

### 3. What is a typical pet fee for a vacation rental in St. Augustine?

Most pet-friendly vacation rentals in St. Augustine charge a non-refundable pet fee between $50 and $150 per stay for one dog (as of Q2 2026). Some properties charge a nightly surcharge of $25 to $50 instead. Additional pets often cost $75 to $100 extra. Confirm the fee structure before booking.

### 4. Are condos in St. Augustine pet-friendly for vacation rentals?

Some condos are, but many are not. Individual condo owners may list a unit as pet-friendly, but the community HOA can override that permission. Resort communities like Ocean Village Club and Barefoot Trace have their own pet policies. Always verify pet rules at the community management level, not just the listing level.

### 5. Do pet-friendly vacation rentals in St. Augustine allow large dogs?

Policies vary widely. Many properties cap pet weight at 25 to 50 pounds. HOA-governed communities frequently exclude specific breeds like pit bulls, Rottweilers, and German Shepherds regardless of weight. Standalone houses are more likely to accept larger dogs. Contact the property manager with your dog's breed and weight before booking.

### 6. What is the cancellation policy if I book a pet-friendly rental and my pet can no longer come?

Cancellation policies for pet-related changes depend on the property and the booking platform. Most non-refundable pet fees cannot be recovered once charged, even if the pet does not stay. Some property managers will waive the pet fee if notified before check-in. Review the cancellation terms at the time of booking.

### 7. Are there long-term pet-friendly vacation rentals in St. Augustine?

Yes. Several property managers in St. Augustine offer monthly rental options for stays of 30 days or more. Long-term pet-friendly rentals are more common among standalone houses than condos. Pet fees for extended stays may be structured differently, often as a monthly charge rather than a per-stay flat fee. Ask about long-term rates directly.

### 8. How do I find a pet-friendly short-term rental in St. Augustine, FL?

Search vacation rental sites using the "pet-friendly" filter combined with your preferred area and dates. Verify the pet policy directly with the property manager before booking. Check for weight limits, breed restrictions, pet fees, and fenced yard availability. Cross-reference the listing details with the community or HOA rules for condos.
