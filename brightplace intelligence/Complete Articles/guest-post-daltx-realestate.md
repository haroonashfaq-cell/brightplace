# Renting in Plano's Legacy Corridor: What DFW Renters Should Know in 2026

The rental market in Plano's Legacy West corridor has shifted. New construction deliveries in 2024 and 2025 added inventory that did not exist two years ago, and the result is a competitive environment where renters have more negotiating power than they have had since the pandemic-era peak. If you are considering the Legacy area, or any Class A suburban community in the DFW metro, here is what the current market actually looks like.

## The Legacy Corridor Is a Renter's Market Right Now

Legacy West and the stretch of Plano running along the Dallas North Tollway between Legacy Drive and Headquarters Drive have absorbed significant new multifamily inventory over the past 18 months. Communities like [Parkside at Legacy in Plano](https://brightplace.ai/knowledgebase/parkside-at-legacy-plano) are competing for tenants against newer deliveries from Cortland, Lantower, and other operators along the same corridor.

For renters, this means concessions. One month free, reduced deposits, and waived admin fees are common across the submarket as of mid-2026. That was not the case in 2022, when vacancy was tight and landlords had no reason to negotiate.

The practical takeaway: if you are touring Legacy corridor apartments, ask about current concessions before you commit. The listed price is rarely the final price in this environment.

## What the Tollway Premium Actually Buys You

Renters pay more in the Legacy corridor than in most DFW suburbs. One-bedroom units in this submarket range from approximately $1,500 to $1,900 per month, and two-bedrooms sit between $2,000 and $2,800. Those prices are 15 to 25 percent above what comparable square footage costs in Frisco or Richardson.

What are you paying for? Walkability to Legacy West retail and dining (Whole Foods, a concentrated restaurant row, Shops at Legacy), proximity to the Toyota, JPMorgan Chase, Liberty Mutual, and HP campuses, and access to DART's CityLine/Bush station for transit into downtown Dallas.

The tradeoff is real, too. The Dallas North Tollway adds $160 to $280 per month in toll costs for daily commuters to downtown, and the interchange at SH-121 creates meaningful congestion during peak hours. Renters who do not work within the Legacy employment cluster should run the commute math before signing.

## The No-Deposit Trend Is Reaching DFW

One shift DFW renters should be tracking: the growth of deposit alternative programs. Traditionally, Texas renters paid one to two months' rent upfront as a security deposit. That could mean $1,500 to $3,600 in cash before you even move in.

A growing number of DFW communities now offer [alternatives to the traditional security deposit](https://brightplace.ai/knowledgebase/homes-for-rent-no-deposit), replacing the lump-sum deposit with a smaller non-refundable fee or a monthly charge. For renters who have the income to qualify but not the savings for a large upfront payment, these programs remove a real barrier.

The catch: deposit alternatives are not free money. The fee you pay is non-refundable, unlike a traditional deposit that comes back if you leave the unit in good condition. For a renter staying 12 months and leaving without damage, a traditional deposit is the better financial outcome. For a renter who needs to preserve cash at move-in, the alternative makes the unit accessible.

Ask the leasing office which option is available and run the numbers both ways before deciding.

## What to Negotiate in a Soft Market

DFW's suburban Class A market is softer than it has been in years. Renters have leverage they may not realize. Here is what to ask for:

**Concessions on rent.** One to two months free on a 12-month lease is common. This reduces your effective monthly rent by 8 to 16 percent. Ask whether the concession applies to the first month, the last month, or is amortized across the term.

**Waived fees.** Application fees ($50 to $85 per adult), admin fees ($150 to $300), and amenity fees are all negotiable in a soft market. Ask which fees can be waived or reduced.

**Shorter lease flexibility.** If you are uncertain about your timeline, ask about 6- or 9-month lease options. The premium for a shorter lease is typically 10 to 15 percent above the 12-month rate, but in a market with elevated vacancy, some operators will reduce or eliminate that premium to fill units.

**Parking.** Garage and covered parking spots that normally carry a $50 to $150 monthly add-on may be included at no cost during a concession period. Ask specifically.

## The Bottom Line for DFW Renters

Plano's Legacy corridor remains one of the strongest rental submarkets in DFW for walkability, employer proximity, and amenity quality. But the supply picture has changed. Renters who treat 2026 as a negotiation, not just a search, will find better terms than they expect.

Tour multiple properties in the same week. Ask about concessions at every stop. Run the full cost comparison, including tolls, parking, and deposit structure, before you sign. The market is doing you a favor right now. Take it.
